# Roost — Player's Manual

**Flap above the lava, unseat rival riders from above, and gather the eggs before they hatch into something worse.**

## The story

High over a bubbling lava pit, mounted riders joust on the backs of great
birds. You ride a long-necked heron; your rivals ride hunched raptors, and
they want you in the lava. Whoever's lance rides *higher* at the moment two
birds collide walks away — the loser bursts into a tumbling egg. Snatch those
eggs before they hatch into fiercer fliers, survive wave after wave, and keep
one eye on the sky: something with a very long beak screeches in now and then.

## Controls

| Playdate | Action |
| --- | --- |
| **D-pad left / right** | Steer |
| **A** *or* **Crank** | Flap (each flap gives an upward push) |
| **A** | Start / play again |
| System menu → **restart** | Return to the title screen |

You never stop falling — tap flap to gain height, hold a direction to build
speed, and let go to glide. The screen **wraps left-to-right**: fly off one
edge and you reappear on the other.

## How to play

Roost is a flap-to-fly aerial joust. You and a flock of rival riders share a
platform arena suspended over lava. When two birds collide, the higher rider
wins:

- **Your lance is higher** → you unseat the rival. It bursts and drops an
  **egg**.
- **Your lance is lower** → you get unseated and lose a life.
- **Dead level** → you bounce off each other, no harm done. Reposition and
  come back from above.

So the whole game is about **winning the altitude**: climb before you clash.

### Eggs

Every rider you unseat leaves a falling egg. Fly into it to collect it for
points. But an egg left alone **hatches in about 9 seconds** — and it hatches
into a bird **one tier tougher** than the one that dropped it. An egg starts
to wobble in its last couple of seconds as a warning. Eggs that fall into the
lava are lost (no hatch, no points).

### Waves

Clear every rider, egg, and pending spawn to finish a wave and move to the
next, tougher one. Each wave sends more birds and a nastier mix of tiers.
Finish a wave **without losing a single life** and you bank a big **survival
bonus**.

### Lives

You start with **3 lives** (shown as small triangles, top-right). Lose your
last life and it's game over. You earn an **extra life every 20,000 points**.
After you spawn or respawn you flash and are briefly invulnerable — use that
moment to get clear.

## Scoring

| Action | Points |
| --- | --- |
| Unseat a **Fledgling** | 500 |
| Unseat a **Harrier** | 750 |
| Unseat a **Roc** | 1,500 |
| Collect an egg | 250 × chain (chain builds up to ×4 within a wave) |
| Catch an egg **in mid-air** | +250 bonus on top |
| Kill the **pterodactyl** (head-on) | 1,000 |
| Clear a wave with **no deaths** | 3,000 survival bonus |
| Extra life | every 20,000 points |

The egg chain resets at the start of each wave, so a clean sweep of eggs in
one wave is worth chasing.

## Enemies & hazards

- **Fledgling** (tier 1, 500 pts) — the common rider. Wanders to random spots
  and rarely hunts you; the easiest clash.
- **Harrier** (tier 2, 750 pts) — faster and smarter. Actively tries to get
  **above** you before it clashes, so it's dangerous to approach carelessly.
- **Roc** (tier 3, 1,500 pts) — fastest and most aggressive (horned helm,
  dark slash on its mount). Also fights for the high ground and closes fast.
- **The Pterodactyl** — a screeching interloper that periodically sweeps
  across the arena, tracking your height. It is **invincible from every angle
  but one**: meet it face-to-face at the same height and your lance goes
  straight into its open beak (1,000 points). Any other contact kills you —
  when you can't line up the head-on, just dodge.
- **The Lava** — the floor is death. Touch it and you lose a life. It also
  swallows rival riders who sink into it (but you get no egg or points for a
  lava kill).

## Tips

1. **Win the height first.** Never clash from below. Flap up to sit above a
   rival, or drop onto one from a higher platform — altitude decides the joust.
2. **Grab eggs fast.** They hatch into a *tougher* bird in ~9 seconds. Catch
   them in mid-air for the +250 bonus, and chain catches within a wave to
   multiply the value up to ×4.
3. **Go for the clean wave.** The 3,000 survival bonus for finishing a wave
   without dying is worth more than most kills — play it safe when you're close.
4. **The pterodactyl is a timing puzzle, not a brawl.** Line up dead-level and
   facing it to spear its beak; otherwise give it a wide berth.
5. **Use the wrap-around.** Being chased by a bird that's above you? Flee off
   the screen edge and pop out the far side to break its approach.
6. **Bait the lava when swarmed.** Rivals that dive too low sink and die on
   their own — you score nothing, but it thins a crowded wave in a pinch.
